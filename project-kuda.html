Kuda Debit Card for Teens

A product concept that extends Kuda Bank's offering to teenage users in Nigeria — addressing financial literacy, parental controls, and safe spending habits for the next generation of digital-native bank customers.

Project Type
Portfolio Concept
Industry
Fintech / Banking
Tools Used
Figma, User Research

The Problem

Most Nigerian teenagers have no access to banking products designed for them. Parents worry about giving children unrestricted access to money, and teens have no safe way to learn financial responsibility. Kuda, already a leading digital bank, had an opportunity to own this underserved segment early.

My Approach

I approached this as a full product discovery exercise:

  • Identified the target users — teenagers aged 13–17 and their parents
  • Mapped out their pain points through secondary research
  • Defined the core jobs-to-be-done for both user groups
  • Designed the key features that would serve both teens and parents

Key Features Designed

  • Teen debit card with spending limits set by parents
  • Parental dashboard to monitor and approve transactions
  • Savings goals feature to teach financial discipline
  • Instant notifications for both parent and teen on every spend

What I Learned

Building for two user groups simultaneously — teens and parents — requires balancing autonomy with oversight. The product had to feel empowering for teens while giving parents enough control to feel safe. That tension shaped every design decision.
